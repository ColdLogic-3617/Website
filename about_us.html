Overall Team

Who are we?

Formed in 2010, FRC Team #3617 or Team Cold Logic as our members prefer to be called is a consortium of local students and mentors who share in the experience of building and competing with robots within the confines of FIRST which serves as our parent organization. Following the January kickoff event MSHS students, teaching staff, parent volunteers, industry mentors, and University staff have six weeks to brainstorm, design, build, and test a single functioning robot to compete against other Michigan high school teams in this year's competition.


Team Cold Logic Mission Statement!

It is our shared vision that participation in FIRST serves as a mechanism for helping students and mentors reach higher levels of personal and academic achievement characteristically attained in competitive events outside of the classroom. Impacts of FRC on students include:

  • Increased understanding of science & technology in everyday life
  • Learning new and practical work-related skills through teamwork
  • Increased value and understanding of teamwork
  • Increase self-confidence
  • Ability to build upon team success while enabling future career interest in science & technology

Compared to similar groupings in terms of backgrounds and achievements in high school, FIRST alumni are significantly more likely to attend college and twice as likely to major in science and engineering fields.


What is FRC?

For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology, or FIRST, is an organization founded by inventor Dean Kamen in 1989 to develop ways to inspire students in engineering and technology fields. The organization is the foundation for the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC), FIRST Lego League, Junior FLL, and FIRST Tech Challenge.

So... what is unique about the FRC program?

FRC has been touted as a varsity sport for the mind. Working to build a robot under strict guidelines, time restrains, and limited resources is about as close to real-world conditions as students can get which in turn helps prepare them for high-tech and high-demand jobs of the future. Other benefits to FRC include:

  • Combining the excitement of sports with the rigors of science and technology
  • Participants play and learn alongside career and educational professionals
  • Students learn how to test and implement sophisticated electronic hardware and software.
  • Competitions serve as a proving ground for over $11 million in college scholarships
  • Competitions redefine the term "winning" by rewarding teams for excellence in design, team spirit, gracious professionalism, safety, and ability to overcome obstacles

Sales and Special Events Team

The team who organizes the fundraising events—such as our light bulb sales. This team is responsible for deciding on dates and times and gathering volunteers to work the events.


Build

Chassis Team

The chassis team forms in the first week of build season. Their job is to build the main chassis, the foundation for our designs. After this is done they split off into other teams.


Build Team

The Build Team consists of those who are trained and willing to use power tools of various size. If you need welding, drilling, cutting, or otherwise assembly done, then these are the ones you should go to.


Programming Electronics and Pneumatics (PEP)

PEP is responsible for all of the code, electronics, and air systems on the robot. Because these are finicky systems, they require extra experience to sort out entirely.


Competition

Pit Crew

The Pit Crew is a small team that knows the inner functions of the robot very well. They are the ones who swap out parts between matches and make last minute repairs. Typically it consists of one PEP member and one Build Team member, with a supervising mentor.


Drive Team

The Drive Team is a set of four people who control the robot while it is on the field. It consists of one Field Coach (coordinator), one Driver (controls movement and gearing), one Button Monkey (controls projectiles, etc.), and one Human Player, whose job varies from year to year.


Scout Platoon

The Scout Platoon is a drafted army of information-gatherers. If there is a stat on another team, you can expect it to be grabbed by them. They relay this information to the Drive and Pit teams between matches so that they can make whatever preparations are needed. They also make the final decision on which teams are in our eliminations alliance.


Community Outreach

Taiwan Exchange

This team periodically video chats with students in Taiwan, helping them better understand spoken English and American culture. It will be expanding to Japan in the near- to mid-future.
