Bruce

2014, v2.3.1

Designed for 2014s game: Aerial Assist.

With this robot, we attempted to combine offensive and defensive elements into a concise package. Playing a soccer-like game we decided to treat our robot much like a midfielder—excellent at offense, capable of defense.

To do this, we created a jaw structure in which to enclose the ball, and a catapult arm to launch it with. This gave us the capability to get it in either goal (low or high), pass it over the truss, and load from the ground. On the defensive end we used a four-motor drivetrain and a variable gearbox, allowing us to both push the competition out of the way and speed across the track at a moments notice.

Like in previous years, we relied heavily on pneumatics to run the robot. The jaw structure was powered by four pneumatic pistons (two synced pairs), and the gearshift was also powered by pneumatics. This forced us to use four storage tanks, a team record. We also had a team record in the number of motor controllers, with a whopping seven. These went to the drivetrain (four CIMs), the launcher-arm (two CIMs), and the loader at the top of the jaw (one bag motor).

The LED lights were controlled by an onboard Arduino unit. This was interfaced with our cRIO via a jury-rigged PWM setup, with the cRIO communicating our team color and whether we were enabled in order to change the color of these lights. It is this system that is the inspiration for our latest project, OpenOffload.
